About e-commerce and technology

Online retailing, electronic funds transfers, supply chain management, Internet marketing, online transaction processing, electronic data exchange (EDI), inventory management systems, and automated data gathering systems are just a few of the technologies that are used in electronic commerce. Electronic commerce (or e-commerce) is the exchange of products and services, the sending of money or data through an electronic network, most often the internet. These business dealings can be between consumers, businesses, or other consumers, as well as between consumers and businesses. E-commerce is powered by the internet. Customers use their own devices to access an online store to browse the selection and place orders for goods or services.



Positive Impacts

Numerous businesses have benefited from or even been established as a result of technology. Businesses all throughout the world have benefited from technology, whether it is through improved productivity, communication, or efficiency.Businesses now find it simpler to compete on a worldwide scale because to technology. Technology's beneficial effects on company will grow as it develops further. Employees can now participate in meetings remotely using video conferencing rather than having to spend time driving to them. Businesses can now track their sales and inventories using software as well.



Negative Impacts

Issues like the invasion of client privacy are being directly caused by online shopping's harmful repercussions. There is a significant security issue when making payments online, which leads to issues like credit card fraud and identity theft. E-commerce is criticized for harming small businesses in addition to customers. Due to people's preference for internet shopping, many small stores close their doors. The start-up costs associated with establishing and promoting an internet business prevent many shops from competing in the competitive world of online buying.
